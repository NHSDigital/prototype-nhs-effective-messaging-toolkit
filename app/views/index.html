Fourth iteration of Effective Messaging Toolkit for Primary Care.

Iterations made following round 3 of toolit co-design workshops:

  • Message senders toolkit checklist
    • added in bullet about using simple language
    • changed linking approach so there is a single link for each section to find out more
    • changed styling on numbered list as requested by Sarah Wilcox (Service Manual team)
    • edited content to make reading age as low as possible
    • Communication channels for messages
      • introduced nested sub-nav for each communication channel (app messages, text messages, etc.)
      • changed terminology for 'message services' to be consistent with NHS App guidance
      • changed terminology to 'batch messages to multiple patients'
      • added content to clarify how messages are sent through the NHS app
      • made 1st para of 'if a patient does not see an app message' clearer
      • included relevant headlines of Scope article within content, rather than linking out (in email section)
      • changed title of sub-heading about sending fewer fragments
      • edited content to make reading age as low as possible
      • Send accessible and inclusive messages
        • added content to explain more about assistive technology and how users can make sure their message content will be understood by users of these technologies
        • added inline examples to show a similar message with a high and low reading age
        • added phone call with a translator as another option on the bullet list in translations section
        • changed trauma-informed quote to clarify that people not experiencing stress and trauma also confused 'appointment' and 'referral' (after discussion with Miriam from Wayfinder)
        • edited content to make reading age as low as possible
      • Send messages that patients trust
        • added a research insight about patients not trusting shortened links
        • clarified that content about asking for DOB is not referring to someone accessing secure messages on Accurx
        • moved GDPR content to the 'send relevant, personalised and appropriate' messages page
        • edited content to make reading age as low as possible
      • Send messages that are appropriate and relevant
        • changed content to refer to clinical system as way to identify groups of patients
        • rephrased content about avoiding sending messages to large groups to make it clearer there is sometimes a need for this
        • added content about potential implications for the patient of including confidential content in a message
        • edited content to make reading age as low as possible
        • moved GDPR content to this page
        • edited content to make reading age as low as possible
