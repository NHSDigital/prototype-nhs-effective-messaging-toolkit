Content guide -

Send effective messages to patients

This guide is for anyone who sends messages to patients in the NHS. It will help you to create and send more effective messages.

1. Choose the right communication channel

Essential

  • Use the NHS App to send non-urgent messages securely at no cost.
  • Send messages in a format that people with a recorded communication need can access.

Best practice

  • If you need to send an SMS, make sure you use as few message fragments as possible to reduce costs.

Find out more about different communication channels for messages.

2. Ensure messages are accessible and inclusive

Essential

  • Use the NHS Content guide to write in short sentences and plain English.
  • Use the NHS content guide when creating new messages to make language inclusive.
  • Make your messages available in alternative formats (also known as accessible formats).
  • Make sure you're familiar with the NHS Accessible Information Standard.

Best practice

  • Make all possible efforts to translate your messages into other languages.

Find out more about sending accessible and inclusive messages.

3. Send messages that patients can trust

Essential

  • Do not use a third-party link shortening service to reduce the length of links.
  • Use an appropriate SenderID (the text that appears instead of a telephone number).
  • Take steps to protect people from spam or phishing.

Best practice

  • Use the NHS Content guide when creating new messages so they have a consistent style.
  • Always ask someone else to check your messages for errors before you send them.

Find out more about sending messages that patients trust.

4. Ensure messages are appropriate and relevant to the recipient

Essential

  • Use the information you have available to identify specific groups of people.
  • Tailor message content so it's relevant to those specific groups.
  • Do not include confidential information, as other people could see messages.
  • Make sure your messages follow UK GDPR and data protection legislation.

Best practice

  • Use voice and tone guidance to write messages that make patients feel cared for.
  • Share general information by putting a banner on your website or a post on social media.

Find out more about sending personalised, appropriate and relevant messages.
