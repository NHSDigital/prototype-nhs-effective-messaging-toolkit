Messaging patients effectively

This guide outlines some essential requirements and best practice guidance for sending effective messages to patients. It's designed for anyone who sends messages to patients in the NHS.

  1. 1

    1 Choose the right communication channel

    Essential

    • Use the NHS App to send non-urgent messages securely at no cost.
    • Send messages in a format that can be accessed by people with a recorded communication need.

    Best practice

    • If you need to send an SMS, make sure you use as few message fragments as possible to reduce costs.
  2. 2

    2 Ensure messages are accessible and inclusive

    Essential

    • Use the NHS Content guide when creating new messages to make language inclusive.
    • Make your messages available in alternative formats (also known as accessible formats).

    Best practice

    • Translate your messages into other languages.
  3. 3

    3 Send messages that patients can trust

    Essential

    • Do not use a third-party link shortening service to reduce the length of link.
    • Use an appropriate SenderID (the text which appears instead of the sending telephone number).
    • Protect people from spam or phishing.
    • Make sure your messages follow UK GDPR and data protection legislation.

    Best practice

    • Use the NHS Content guide when creating new messages so they have a consistent style.
    • Always ask someone else to check your messages for errors before you send them.
