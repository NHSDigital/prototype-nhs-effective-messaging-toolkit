Make standard messages accessible

Aim to make all the text messages, emails and app messages you send accessible to people who:

  • use assistive technology, like a screenreader
  • have a low level of literacy
  • have low numeracy
  • have a learning difficulty or autism

This will reduce the number of messages you need to send in accessible formats. These are also known as accessible formats.

Use our content guide to write messages that are:

  • clear and in in plain English, so someone with a reading age of 9 to 11 years can understand them
  • concise, with short sentences and paragraphs
  • in the active voice, not passive voice
    •

    Specific pages of the content guide tell you how to:

    • use words and phrases that patients understand
    • use formatting and punctuation
    • write numbers, measurements, dates and times
    • use links

    The GOV.UK style guide also has a useful list of words to avoid, with plain English alternatives.

    The messages you send might include links to your website. Find out how to make your website usable and accessible for patients.
